## Opportunity Summary

Remy is an AI product agent that produces polished, production-grade business applications from plain-language descriptions and conversations. Remy fills the commercially-underserved middle of the AI software creation market. On one side are prototype generators like Lovable and Bolt, which produce frontends and demos but struggle to build real applications with any sort of depth or production-readiness. On the other are engineering-focused coding agents like Claude Code and Codex, which accelerate and empower existing engineering teams and require developer fluency.

The ICP is the non-technical business operator at mid-market and SMB companies (ops, finance, HR, revenue operations), a buyer for whom no other platform today produces real, owned, full-stack applications on demand. The US SAM is estimated at $15-25B. During Remy's alpha, an institutional customer has used Remy to build a full fund-management application set to run that organization's fund operations — one concrete example among hundreds of alpha builds, included here as a depth signal for the kind of serious software alpha users can produce on the platform.

Remy works. The category question has shifted from "can this be built" to "who captures this segment." The bet: Remy becomes the dominant product agent for the business-software market, reached via PLG into the technical-PM wedge and org-level expansion from there into the broader operator base. Platform licensing into SaaS incumbents and strategic exit optionality are amplifiers that compound the primary motion when they land — not fallback paths. See [Strategy → What wins](./07_strategy-and-gtm.md#what-wins-the-conviction) for the conviction case.

## Corporate Structure

**Wooster Labs, Inc.** (Delaware C-corp) is the new entity. Remy is the product and brand. The entity is fresh, formed at spinout from **GoMeta, Inc.**, where the same two founders have been operating together since 2018. The spinout is a structural reset — a clean cap table and a clean operating mandate for Remy, with the prior chapters of GoMeta's history acknowledged rather than carried.

### The GoMeta arc, in plain terms

Two distinct product chapters sit behind us.

**2018–2023 — Koji.** Link-in-bio platform for the creator economy. GoMeta raised roughly $36M across two rounds (~$10M Series A in June 2020, $20M Series B led by Jump Capital in January 2022) and grew Koji to approximately 700,000 creators and ~70 staff. The link-in-bio category consolidated under Linktree, and in December 2023 Linktree acquired Koji on terms that did not return capital to GoMeta's investors. Linktree sunset the consumer Koji product on January 31, 2024; significant portions of the underlying technology remain in active use inside Linktree today.

**2024–2026 — MindStudio, then Remy.** After the Koji acquisition, the team continued operating GoMeta on its remaining cash, without raising additional outside capital, to build MindStudio. MindStudio shipped as a drag-and-drop AI workflow builder, found real customers and real revenue, but did not scale to venture proportions. The category (n8n / Zapier / Make-shaped tools) was being structurally compressed by the same frontier-model maturation that has since opened the Remy opportunity — when an agent can read user intent in plain language and write code that does what a visual workflow would have done, the visual builder is a worse abstraction. The team did not double down on that form. They used the MindStudio period to harden the platform substrate underneath and to figure out what the agent-generation form of the product should be. That work produced Remy. MindStudio is being sunset; its substrate carries forward as the runtime Remy compiles against.

It's been roughly four years between Koji's last round (January 2022) and Remy's Seed (now). Prior investors have held paper across two product cycles without a return event. We engage that history directly in [Risks → Predecessor history](./06_risks-and-downside.md#5-predecessor-history) — it's the load-bearing diligence question and the docs answer it head-on.

### Why a new entity for Remy

Wooster Labs exists because Remy warrants a structural reset, not another product release inside GoMeta:

- A clean cap table makes the Seed mechanics straightforward, with the Koji-era history acknowledged rather than embedded.
- The operating mandate is fully on Remy. No parent product runs in the background.
- The platform substrate built across the prior chapters — 200+ AI models, 1,000+ integrations, sandbox execution, managed databases, auth, deployment, SOC 2 Type 1/2, GDPR — transferred to Wooster Labs at spinout. That substrate is the durable asset from the GoMeta years and the head start Remy is built on.

GoMeta's existing investors rolled forward into Wooster Labs via a new pre-seed SAFE issued at spinout. They watched both prior chapters play out from inside the cap table and chose to come along on the next bet on Remy-specific terms. The Seed round being raised sits on top of that SAFE on an otherwise clean cap table.

**Remy is an alpha product and is pre-revenue.** The platform substrate Remy compiles against has been in enterprise production for years — useful as a credibility signal for the maturity of the infrastructure, but those users are not transferring as Remy revenue.

We're happy to go deeper on any of this — Koji's commercial trajectory, MindStudio's specifics, the spinout mechanics, the SAFE terms. The summary above is the load-bearing context; the rest is detail available on request.
